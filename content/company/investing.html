Investing

Investing in a social business is investing directly to make the world a better place. Social business delinks ownership from personal financial gain and puts all the stakeholders on the same playing field.

We call it donations on steroids.

Motivations

We believe strongly that the way to fix the current problems with research is through business methods and practices. The problems exist because of business and we need to fix them with business. We are working with an economic system here and we need economic solutions.

Social business, especially as defined by Muhammad Yunus, give the best set of motivations for fixing these problems.

As a social business, we go beyond that minimum legal requirement by establishing a non-dividend stock policy and reinvesting 100% of our profits in ways that will promote open research, build up the community, improve our products and services, or in some other way entirely---whatever will further our mission the most and maximize our social impact in the world.

There is somethat that happens to an investor when there is the complete abdication of financial profit. Selfishness and greed leave, and the investor is concerned complete with the company mission. Other motivations for being involved in the company come to the surface, and the investor feels part of the team and like they are part of something bigger than themself.

vs non-profit Stakeholder value replaces the shareholder paradigm. the social business offers businesspeople an exciting opportunity to leverage their own business skills and creativity to solve social problems.

The Exit

Legally, we're organized as a benefit corporation, which means that we're required to create a material positive impact on society and the environment. Yet we were not satisfied with the standard options for businesses set up to do good in the world. It seemed that many businesses started out doing good, but became corrupted as rounds of funding progressed.

As we looked at other companies that started out well but then tanked, we realized the problem was the exit. We needed a way to exit that did not induce selfish remonstrations by investors. One aspect that we needed was for our business model to be structured around openness. But this was not enough to be sure that the future goodness of the company was somewhat guaranteed. It's all about motivations.
