An Open Approach

The real purpose of an open approach is to enable collaboration. Everyone knows that many hands make light work. Open is the way to make the most progress. Closed solutions may have some short-term gains, but open makes the most long-term sense. In order to reap the rewards of an open approach, we need to be willing to share what we are doing.

  • Open Research
  • Open Source
  • Open Business

Open Research

Open is the future of research. Open research involves collaboration throughout the research process, not just at the end when the conclusions are written. It is done in a spirit of mutual cooperation. Researchers from different locations and backgrounds work together and come to better conclusions more quickly. Transparency allows others to view the true nature and caliber of another's research and even build on top of it.

Open Source

Source code that is open and available to be used freely will be improved upon more often than the closed alternative. We are strong supporters of open-source software. Our entire infrastructure is built upon it. We contribute much of the source code that we write to the community as well. We hope that others can benefit from this code and contribute back!

Open Business

We are in business to help make your research easier. We figure that if you are not happy with what we are doing, we are doing something wrong. We think the best way to keep our customers happy is to involve them in some of our business decisions. You may say, "Isn't that supposed to be secret?" Well, we have thought a lot about that. We have come to the conclusion that we will be a better company if we are transparent and involve our customers in the decision-making process. With modern technology, why not do it this way? It's an experiment. We don't know how well it will work, but we think it will be great.

We can't please everyone, but by open-sourcing other needs can be met by the community.
