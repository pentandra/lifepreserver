An Open Approach

Everyone knows that many hands make light work. Open is the way to make the most progress. Closed solutions may produce some short-term gains, but open makes the most long-term sense. In order to reap the rewards of collaboration, we need to be willing to share what we are doing.

  • Open Research
  • Open Source
  • Open Business

Open Research

S: Speed of discovery. Future research will be based on solid ground. We will never run out of researchable stuff W: Some people just want to keep it to themselves for various reasons. Privacy. We want to encourage openness. O: The internet. mobile devies. wireless internet. T: Publishers that don't do anything except publish and lock up other people's research for money. They don't just not add value, they remove value.

This is called Open Research. Even within one discipline,

Open Source

S: Community can benefit from and improve a common code base. We have benefited ourselves from many open-source projects and we want to give back and make the world a better place. W: Someone could take it and use it and compete with us? Some things are better NOT open-sourced. Imagine if twitter open-sourced their platform and everyone and his dog hosted their own twitter network. How useful would that be? It just doesn't make sense. Parts dealing with collaboration need to be centralized, or at least federated. O: T:

We believe the future of software is open source. We are involved in several open source projects

Open Business

S: We are truly connected to the community. We can better tailor our efforts to them, not to what we think they want. Gives the community skin in the game and a willingness to give back so we can work together. There can be understanding between us and our customers. W: Fighting about stuff. Slow decisions. If we don't make it completely open, it won't feel open. We need someone to make the final call. O: Respect and us an opportunity to respect them as we work together. T: Someone chooses not to show respect. That hurts the whole group.

We are in business to help make your research easier. We figure that if you are not happy with what we are doing, we are doing something wrong. We think the best way to keep our customers happy is to involve them in some of our business decisions. You may say, "Isn't that supposed to be secret?" Well, we have thought a lot about that. We have come to the conclusion that we will be a better company if we are transparent and involve our customers in the decision-making process. With modern technology, why not do it this way? It's an experiment. We don't know how well it will work, but we think it will be great.

We can't please everyone, but by open-sourcing other needs can be met by the community.
